Computation Methodology & Formulas
1N₆₀ Correction (Energy Normalization)
N₆₀ = N_field × (ηH/60) × ηB × ηS × ηR
ηH = hammer efficiency (%) | ηB = borehole diameter factor | ηS = sampler type factor | ηR = rod length factor
Rod LengthηR
< 3 m0.75
3 – 4 m0.80
4 – 6 m0.85
6 – 10 m0.95
> 10 m1.00
Skempton (1986); Bowles, Foundation Analysis and Design (1996)
2Effective Vertical Stress (σ'v)
σv = Σ(γᵢ × Δzᵢ) [total vertical stress]
u = γw × zw where γw = 9.81 kN/m³ [pore water pressure]
σ'v = σv − u [effective vertical stress]
Das, Principles of Geotechnical Engineering (8th ed.)
3Overburden Correction Factor (CN)
CN = √(Pa / σ'v) [Liao & Whitman, 1986]
CN ≤ 2.0
where Pa = 100 kPa (atmospheric pressure reference)
Liao & Whitman (1986), J. of Geotechnical Engineering, ASCE
4Corrected SPT Value N₁(₆₀)
N₁(₆₀) = CN × N₆₀
Youd et al. (2001), ASCE J. Geotechnical & Geoenvironmental Engineering
5Relative Density Dr (Sands)
Dr = √(N₁(₆₀) / Cd) × 100%
Cd = 0.23 (fine sand) | 0.25 (medium sand) | 0.30 (coarse sand)
Skempton (1986), Geotechnique
6Friction Angle φ' (Sands)
φ' = 27.1 + 0.3N₆₀ − 0.00054(N₆₀)² [degrees]
Wolff (1989), as cited in Das (2014)
φ' = tan⁻¹[N₆₀/(12.2 + 20.3σ'v/Pa)]^0.34 [Peck et al.]
Peck, Hanson & Thornburn (1974)
7Undrained Shear Strength Su (Clays)
Su = 0.29 × σ'v × (N₆₀ × Pa/σ'v)^0.72 [kPa]
Kulhawy & Mayne (1990), EPRI EL-6800
8Allowable Bearing Capacity qa
For Sand (Meyerhof, 1956, modified):
qa = (N₆₀ / 0.08) × [(B + 0.3)/B]² × Fd [kPa, B in m]
where Fd = 1 + 0.33(Df/B) ≤ 1.33 [depth factor]
For Clay: qa = 5.14Su(1 + sc + dc − ic)/FS
FS = 3.0 | Settlement ≤ 25mm assumed
Meyerhof (1956); Bowles (1996); Das (2014)
9Liquefaction Potential — CRR & CSR
CSR = 0.65 × (σv/σ'v) × amax/g × rd
rd = 1.0 − 0.00765z (z ≤ 9.15 m) [stress reduction coeff.]
CRR₇.₅ = 1/[34 − N₁(₆₀)] + N₁(₆₀)/135 + 50/[10N₁(₆₀)+45]² − 1/200
MSF = 10^2.24 / (Mw^2.56) [Magnitude Scaling Factor]
FS_liq = CRR₇.₅ × MSF / CSR
FS_liq < 1.0 → LIQUEFIABLE | ≥ 1.0 → Non-liquefiable
Youd et al. (2001); Idriss & Boulanger (2008)
